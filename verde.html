Salsa Verde

Salsa verde is the name given to distinct green colored salsas, generally made with tomatillos, garlic, onions, green chiles, and cilantro. Made throughout the country of Mexico, there are many variations; it can be made raw, cooked, or or fried. Raw salsa verde is made by blending the ingredients in a molcajete or blender. The consistency can vary between smooth and textured. Cooked salsa verde is made by boiling the ingredients to later blend them. It is used as a "table salsa" and can be used as a base in recipes such as enchiladas verdes, chilaquiles verdes, and entomatados. On some occasions this salsa can be fried in oil or lard. Fried salsa verde is made by first frying the ingredients and then grinding them in the molcajete. It is a peculiar salsa because the ingredients are not completely cooked. It is very liked in the central states of Mexico for the smokey flavor the tomato acquires when fried.

Ingredients

  • 2 lbs Husked Tomatillos
  • 8 to 12 Serrano Chiles
  • 1/2 Onion, peeled
  • 2 Garlic Cloves
  • 1 Lime
  • Cilantro
  • Salt

Preperation

  1. In a comal, heat oil until shimmering but not smoking. Add tomatillos, chilies, onion (cut side down) and garlic. Cook over medium-high heat, turning often, until vegetables are browned, turning to black, and seared on all sides.
  2. Carefully transfer all the contents from the comal into the jar of a blender. Let cool for about 30 minutes.
