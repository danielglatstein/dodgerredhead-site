Salsa Macha

A salsa casera made in a paste consistency. It is made with fried Chipotle, ground in a molcajete with garlic and salt. Fried peanut can be added and ground in the molcajete. Typically eaten on a tortilla or slice of bread, it is prepared in the state of Veracruz. Salsa macha can be found in paste or liquid form, the liquid form containing olive oil. After mixing them, the salsa separates and the paste of chiles settles the the bottom. It is possible to use only the separated liquid, or to remix it and use the thicker salsa.

Ingredients

  • 2 oz Dried Chipotle Chiles
  • 2 cups Olive Oil
  • 1/3 cup Raw Unsalted Peanuts
  • 4 Garlic Cloves
  • 2 tbl Seasame Seeds
  • 1 tsp Salt
  • 2 tbl Brown Sugar
  • 3 tbl Apple Cider Vinegar

Preperation

  1. Set a large heavy skillet over medium heat and add the oil. Once the oil is hot add the garlic cloves. Stir until they are fragrant. Add the chipotle chiles and peanuts, stir for about two minutes. Add the sesame seeds, stir and continue to fry for about a minute. Remove from heat.
  2. Carefully transfer all the contents from the skillet into the jar of a blender. Let cool for about 30 minutes.
  3. Add the salt, sugar and vinegar. Process until smooth, starting with low speed and building up to high speed. Pour into a container, let cool and refrigerate if the salsa will not be used that day. After a couple hours the salsa will seperate, mix as you wish before serving.
